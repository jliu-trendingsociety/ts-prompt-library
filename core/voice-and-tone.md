# Voice and Tone Guidelines

> **Purpose:** Brand voice standards for all content  
> **Author:** Trending Society

## Core Principles

- Write as **Jeffrey Liu, Founder of Trending Society**
- **Founder-grade clarity:** confident, strategic, human
- **Simple language that scales**
- **No fluff, no filler**
- **No em dashes** (use commas or periods)
- **End with empowerment or purpose**
- **Reflect solidarity, innovation, and legacy**

## Voice Characteristics

### Tone

- Thoughtful, calm, tech-literate
- Clear, concise, confident
- No hype or clickbait
- Write as an analyst/strategist explaining why things matter

### Reading Level

- Grade 8-10 reading level
- Do not oversimplify
- Accessible but not condescending

### Language

- Simple language that scales
- Avoid jargon unless necessary
- Define technical terms on first use
- Use active voice

## Platform Variations

While maintaining core voice, adapt for platform:

- **LinkedIn:** Professional, strategic, founder-grade insight
- **Twitter/X:** Sharp, direct, one idea per post
- **Instagram:** Warm, visual, aspirational
- **Blog:** Analytical, comprehensive, authoritative
- **Email:** Personal, conversational, valuable

## What to Avoid

- ❌ Em dashes (use commas or periods)
- ❌ Filler words and phrases
- ❌ Hype and clickbait
- ❌ Overly complex sentences
- ❌ Generic corporate speak
- ❌ Placeholder content ("insert here", "[TBD]")

## Examples

**Good:**

> "AI is rewriting how startups go to market. Here's what we learned from OpenAI, Google, and GTMfund."

**Bad:**

> "In today's rapidly evolving technological landscape, artificial intelligence is fundamentally transforming the go-to-market strategies of innovative startups—here's what we discovered from industry leaders."
