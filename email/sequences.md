## Email Specifications

| Email Type       | Length        | Frequency           | Primary Goal                        |
| ---------------- | ------------- | ------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Welcome sequence | 300-500 words | Daily for 5-7 days  | Build trust, set expectations       |
| Nurture flow     | 200-400 words | Weekly              | Deliver value, build relationship   |
| Newsletter       | 400-800 words | Weekly/biweekly     | Inform, engage, maintain connection |
| Launch sequence  | 300-600 words | Daily during launch | Convert to sale                     |
| Re-engagement    | 150-300 words | One-time            | Win back inactive subscribers       |

## Voice and Tone for Email

**Trending Society email voice:**

- Direct and personal (write to one person)
- Confident but not salesy
- Founder-to-founder tone
- No hype, no manipulation
- Respect the inbox (every email should earn its open)

**Avoid:**

- "Hey friend!" or overly casual openers
- Countdown timers and false scarcity
- Walls of text with no breaks
- Multiple CTAs competing for attention
- Em dashes (use commas or periods)

## Subject Line Formulas

| Formula          | Example                                    |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Curiosity gap    | "The one thing I'd change"                 |
| Direct benefit   | "How to cut your content time in half"     |
| Question         | "Are you overcomplicating this?"           |
| Personal/story   | "I almost quit last Tuesday"               |
| News hook        | "What OpenAI's new tool means for you"     |
| Number + outcome | "3 automations that save me 10 hours/week" |
| Contrarian       | "Stop creating content"                    |

**Subject line rules:**

- Under 50 characters ideal (mobile preview)
- No ALL CAPS
- No excessive punctuation!!!
- Preview text should complement, not repeat

## Welcome Sequence Template (5 Emails)

### Email 1: The Arrival (Day 0)

**Subject:** You're in. Here's what happens next.
**Preview text:** Plus the one thing I wish someone told me earlier.

```
[FIRST NAME],

Welcome to [Newsletter/Community Name].

You're now part of [number] [founders/creators/operators] who get [core benefit] delivered to their inbox.

Here's what to expect:

Every [frequency], I send [content type]. No fluff. No filler. Just [specific value].

But first, I want to share something that took me [time] to figure out:

[One key insight, 2-3 sentences. Make it valuable enough to screenshot.]

This idea changed how I approach [topic]. More on this in the coming emails.

For now, hit reply and tell me: what's the one thing you're trying to figure out right now?

I read every response.

[Sign-off],
[Name]

P.S. [Optional: link to best resource, quick win, or social proof]
```

---

### Email 2: The Origin Story (Day 1)

**Subject:** Why I started this
**Preview text:** The real reason, not the PR version.

```
[FIRST NAME],

Most people find me because of [known work/content/product].

But that's not how this started.

[2-3 paragraphs: authentic origin story. What problem did you face? What did you try? What breakthrough happened?]

That experience taught me: [core lesson that drives your work].

It's why I [current mission]. And it's why you're getting these emails.

Tomorrow, I'll share [tease next email content].

[Sign-off],
[Name]
```

---

### Email 3: The Quick Win (Day 2)

**Subject:** Try this today
**Preview text:** Takes 10 minutes. Works immediately.

```
[FIRST NAME],

I promised value. Here's something you can use today.

[Describe one actionable tactic, framework, or tool in 3-5 short paragraphs. Be specific enough that they can implement it without needing anything else.]

Why this works:
[Brief explanation, 1-2 sentences]

I've seen [result/outcome] from this approach. Try it and let me know how it goes.

Tomorrow: [tease next email]

[Sign-off],
[Name]
```

---

### Email 4: The Belief Shift (Day 3)

**Subject:** The myth that keeps people stuck
**Preview text:** I believed this too. Then I saw the data.

```
[FIRST NAME],

There's a common belief in [your space]:

"[Common misconception or limiting belief]"

I used to think this too. Then I [experience that changed your mind].

The truth:
[Reframe the belief with evidence or insight]

This matters because [implication for the reader].

The people who break through aren't doing more. They're thinking differently about [topic].

Which brings me to tomorrow's email, where I'll [tease the CTA email].

[Sign-off],
[Name]
```

---

### Email 5: The Invitation (Day 4-5)

**Subject:** Ready to go deeper?
**Preview text:** Only if this resonates.

```
[FIRST NAME],

Over the past few days, I've shared:
- [Key point from email 1]
- [Key point from email 3]
- [Key point from email 4]

If any of this resonated, you might be wondering: what's next?

Here's what I offer:

[Brief description of product/service, focused on transformation, not features]

It's for people who [describe ideal customer situation].

[Social proof: one testimonial, result, or case study snippet]

If that sounds like you: [CTA with link]

If not, no pressure. These emails will keep coming with [ongoing value].

Either way, I'm glad you're here.

[Sign-off],
[Name]
```

## Launch Sequence Template (5 Emails)

### Email 1: Announcement

**Subject:** Something new is here
**Preview text:** Built this for people like you.

```
[Open with why you built it]
[What it is, one sentence]
[Who it's for]
[Primary benefit]
[CTA to learn more / join waitlist / buy]
[P.S. with scarcity or bonus if authentic]
```

### Email 2: The Problem

**Subject:** The real cost of [problem]
**Preview text:** It's not what you think.

```
[Describe the problem vividly]
[Agitate: what happens if it continues]
[Introduce solution as the answer]
[CTA]
```

### Email 3: The Proof

**Subject:** "It actually worked"
**Preview text:** From [customer name] last week.

```
[Lead with testimonial or case study]
[Explain what they did and what happened]
[Connect it back to the reader's situation]
[CTA]
```

### Email 4: The Objection

**Subject:** "But what if [objection]?"
**Preview text:** I hear this a lot. Here's the truth.

```
[Name the common objection directly]
[Address it honestly]
[Provide evidence or reframe]
[CTA]
```

### Email 5: The Close

**Subject:** Last chance for [offer/bonus/price]
**Preview text:** Doors close at midnight.

```
[Remind them what's at stake]
[Recap the offer]
[Final testimonial or proof point]
[Strong CTA]
[Clear deadline]
```

## Quality Gate

Before outputting email copy, verify:

☐ Subject line under 50 characters
☐ Preview text complements (doesn't repeat) subject
☐ Opening line earns the next line
☐ One clear CTA per email
☐ No walls of text (short paragraphs, white space)
☐ No em dashes
☐ Tone is direct and personal, not salesy
☐ Value delivered before any ask
☐ Mobile-friendly formatting

## Email Metrics to Reference

When writing, optimize for:

| Metric      | Good Benchmark | How Copy Affects It             |
| ----------- | -------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Open rate   | 30-40%         | Subject line + preview text     |
| Click rate  | 3-5%           | CTA clarity + value alignment   |
| Reply rate  | 1-2%           | Personal tone + clear questions |
| Unsubscribe | Under 0.5%     | Relevance + frequency           |
